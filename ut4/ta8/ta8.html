Exercise 2

The graph shows a distribution of data in three well-defined classes according to the "petal_width" and "petal_length" attributes. It also shows that the classes "iris-versicolor" and "iris-virginica" are not as separated, meaning that there are mixed data and care must be taken with the chosen value of "k" when using the k-nn algorithm.

First of all, the data should be normalized to fit on the same scale and thus create a feasible model. The "id" column could be eliminated as it does not add value to the prediction.

k-nn:

  • k: Determines the number of closest neighbors to consider when making the prediction.
  • Weighted voting: The distances between examples are also taken into account when making the prediction. This helps determine how much each neighbor will weigh depending on its distance from the example. The closer, the more weight.

Types of measurement:

  • Mixed: Calculates distances in nominal and numerical attributes.
  • Nominal: Calculates distances only in nominal attributes.
  • Numerical: Calculates distances only in numerical attributes.
  • Bregman divergences: More generic approaches that do not comply with the triangle inequality or symmetry.
